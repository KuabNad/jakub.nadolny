About me

Galaxy evolution, star formation, and the interstellar medium.

I am an assistant professor at Adam Mickiewicz University in Poznań, Poland. My research focuses on galaxy evolution, with particular interest in the physical processes that regulate star formation, interstellar gas, dust, and galaxy quenching. Currently I am working on protoclusters, structures that will become galaxy clusters.

Research

Current Projects

Galaxy Evolution and Quenching

Studying how galaxies change over cosmic time through star formation, structural transformation, quenching, feedback, morphology, and environmental effects.

Interstellar Medium

Investigating gas and dust in galaxies, including the mechanisms that remove, heat, or consume the material needed to form stars.

Protoclusters

Investigating dense structures in the early Universe that are expected to evolve into massive galaxy clusters.
